Health Score & Insights

How is your score calculated?

Your V-Med Health Score is a numerical representation of your medical record's reliability and completeness. Having a high score ensures that emergency responders and new doctors get an accurate, trusted view of your clinical status.

📊 Profile Completeness (40%)
Evaluates how well your identity details (ABHA, Aadhaar, DOB, Blood Group) and contact information are filled. A complete profile is vital for identity verification.
📈 Medical Activity (30%)
Factors in your recent clinical visits, active medications, and consistency in tracking vitals. regular updates show an active commitment to health.
🛡️ Data Integrity (30%)
The gold standard. Data uploaded or verified by a registered Doctor carries a higher trust weight (3x) compared to self-logged entries.

Knowledge Base & FAQs

❓ Why did my score drop?
Scores can fluctuate if your medical records become "stale" (e.g., no vitals logged for 3 months) or if you add new data that hasn't been verified by a doctor yet. Verified records are the best way to keep your score high.
❓ Is a higher score always better?
Yes. A high score means your V-Med profile is a "Trusted Digital Twin." It reduces diagnostic errors during new consultations and helps in faster processing for medical insurance or emergency care.
❓ Can I delete unverified data?
You can manage and delete your self-uploaded records at any time. However, to maintain clinical integrity, documents verified by a Doctor (with their clinical ID) are locked and cannot be deleted by the patient.
❓ Who can see my Health Score?
Your health data is private. Only you, and the specific doctors with whom you share your unique V-Med ID, can view your medical summary and health score.
